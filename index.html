Blue Boat

Morning

The morning greets a wondering soul. The day of all days is here. Alas, off to seize the morning.

  • Where to go?
  • Where the wind blows.

Tethered

The sands are many and the land vast. But, to the dock, as in often times, the wonderer turns the course. And as he walks, his feet tangle in the ropes. Tethered, yet, now free.

  • Untie it?
  • To never be tethered.

Blue Boat

The wonderer slips inside the boat, blue as the morning, and listens to the water. There were oars. But they weren't needed. Only dreams. And the boat begins to move.

  • Where?
  • A wonder.

Lone Sailing

He sings, "Though as a lone sailor, there's a place for me...out there." By and by the waves smoothly roll and his path is sure.

  • And the ocean knows?
  • To the dreamer's dream.

Found

To the eastern shore, upon the white sands. The journey ends. His rest is found.

  • To where has the wonderer gone?
  • Where ever the blue boat takes him.
  • Where his dreams come true.

Dream

There's always a dream to live. There's always a place to find. There's always that mile to walk and the mountain to climb.

  • What's your dream?
  • Dream it.
